Frigidaire portable ice maker: honest review

2025-05-20 · Reviews

Here’s the thing:

Frigidaire portable ice maker: honest review

When I first pulled the Frigidaire EFIC108 out of its box, I had one thought: this looks like it belongs in a nicer kitchen than mine. Fun fact: that brushed stainless steel finish gives it a premium vibe that most countertop ice makers just don’t have. But looks only get you so far. I’ve been testing this thing for three weeks now, and I want to break down what’s actually worth your money and what’s just marketing. Frigidaire is a name most people recognize from refrigerators, dishwashers, and other full-size appliances. Here’s the thing: that brand recognition carries weight. You see the Frigidaire logo and you assume quality. Fair enough. But does it translate to their portable ice maker lineup? OK so let’s find out. ## First impressions and build quality

The EFIC108 comes in a few color options, but the stainless steel version is the one most people go for. Real talk: i picked it up for around $110, which puts it in the mid-range category for portable ice makers. Here’s the thing I need to be upfront about. That “stainless steel” exterior? Worth mentioning: it’s a stainless steel wrap over a plastic body. It’s not a full metal construction. I don’t think that’s a dealbreaker at this price point, but you should know what you’re getting. The finish does resist fingerprints better than I expected, and it looks good sitting on the counter next to my coffee maker and toaster. It blends in. The lid on top is a clear window so you can watch the ice form, which is a nice touch. The controls are simple. OK so you get a power button, an ice size selector, and indicator lights. No fancy digital displays. No app connectivity. Just buttons that do what they say they’ll do. I actually prefer this approach. Now, the water reservoir holds about 1.5 liters, and the ice basket can store roughly 1.5 pounds of ice at a time. If you need to compare this against other options, check out my list of the [best portable ice makers]9 best portable ice makers for a broader picture. ## Ice production: how fast and how much? Frigidaire claims the EFIC108 can produce up to 26 pounds of ice per day. In my testing, that number is optimistic but not wildly off. Thing is, i got closer to 22-23 pounds in a full day of continuous operation, which is still plenty for most situations. The first batch of ice takes about 6 to 8 minutes depending on which size you select. Quick note: that’s standard for bullet-style ice makers in this range. After the machine warms up and gets into a rhythm, batches come a bit faster. I was pulling out fresh ice every 6 minutes or so on the small setting. You get two ice sizes: small and large. So both are the bullet shape with the hollow center that you see on most portable ice makers. The small ones are good for drinks where you want faster cooling and don’t mind quicker melting. The large ones hold up better in a glass of whiskey or a tall lemonade. Neither size is going to win any beauty contests. And they’re functional. They cool your drink. Now, that’s the job. One thing I noticed is that on humid days, the machine seems to work a little harder and production slows down slightly. This is normal for any portable ice maker, but it’s worth mentioning if you plan to use this outdoors or in a warm garage. ## Noise level

This is where a lot of portable ice makers lose points, and the Frigidaire is… Fine. Not quiet. Not loud. Real talk: just fine. When the compressor kicks on, there’s a steady hum that I’d compare to a small window fan on low. You’ll hear it if the room is quiet, but it won’t drown out conversation. Honestly, i measured it at roughly 45 decibels from about three feet away, which is in line with most competitors. If you’re sensitive to noise or plan to use this in a bedroom or small studio apartment, you’ll notice it. Honestly, in a kitchen with other ambient noise, it fades into the background pretty quickly. The ice dropping into the basket makes a clinking sound every few minutes. There’s no way around that. Now, ice falls, ice makes noise. It’s not jarring, but if you’re a light sleeper and this thing is running in the next room, you might want to turn it off at night. ## The self-cleaning function

The EFIC108 has a self-cleaning cycle, and I’m glad it does. Portable ice makers can develop mineral buildup and funky smells if you don’t maintain them. Here’s the thing: with the self-cleaning function, you just add water, press the clean button, and let it run for about 10 minutes. I wouldn’t call it a deep clean. You’ll still want to manually wipe down the interior every couple of weeks and descale it with a vinegar solution once a month if you’ve hard water. But the self-cleaning function is great for quick maintenance between uses. It’s one of those features that seems small until you’ve owned a machine without it. ## Day-to-Day use

I’ve used the EFIC108 in a few different scenarios over the past few weeks. For daily use making iced coffee and keeping a water pitcher cold, it’s been reliable. I fill the reservoir in the morning and have ice within 10 minutes. No complaints there. Fun fact: for a small gathering I hosted (about 8 people), it kept up reasonably well. I had to refill the water reservoir twice over the course of a few hours, and the ice basket filled up about every 30 to 40 minutes. If you’re throwing a bigger party, you’d want to pre-make ice and store it in a cooler, then use the machine to keep topping it off throughout the event. And the machine has sensors that stop ice production when the basket is full and alert you when the water runs low. Both worked correctly in my testing. No overflows, no dry runs. Look, basic stuff, but it works. At $110, the Frigidaire EFIC108 sits in a crowded space. Honestly, you’ve got the [Silonn]Silonn ice maker review, which comes in cheaper and delivers similar performance. There’s the [Euhomy]Euhomy ice maker review, which offers a bit more capacity for a similar price. Both are solid machines. Where the Frigidaire stands out is brand trust and aesthetics. If you care about having a recognized name on your appliance and you want something that doesn’t look cheap on your countertop, the EFIC108 delivers on both fronts. The stainless steel finish genuinely looks better than most competitors in this range. Worth mentioning: where it falls short is value. And you’re getting roughly the same ice-making performance as machines that cost $30 to $40 less. The internal components, the ice production rate, the ice quality… It’s all comparable to what you’d get from a lesser-known brand. You’re paying a premium for the Frigidaire name. Whether that premium is worth it depends on how much brand recognition matters to you. ## What i don’t love

No machine is perfect. Here’s what bugged me about the EFIC108. So the ice melts back into the reservoir pretty quickly if you don’t use it. Since there’s no built-in freezer (no portable ice maker has one at this price), the ice starts softening within 30 to 45 minutes of sitting in the basket. The machine does recycle the meltwater and turn it back into ice, which is smart, but it means you need to move your ice to a freezer or cooler if you want to stockpile it. The water reservoir opening is a bit narrow, making it slightly awkward to pour water in from a pitcher. A wider opening or a removable reservoir would have been a nice design improvement. And while the exterior looks great, the interior is all plastic. So it’s food-safe plastic and it’s easy to clean, but it doesn’t match the premium exterior aesthetic. That’s a nitpick, I know. But at $110, I feel like I can nitpick. ## Who should buy this? And the Frigidaire EFIC108 is a good fit if you want a reliable portable ice maker from a brand you trust and you don’t mind paying a small premium for that peace of mind. It’s a good fit for kitchens where the appliance will be visible, because it genuinely looks good. And it’s a solid choice for moderate daily ice needs. It’s not the best fit if you’re on a tight budget and just need ice. You can get the same functional performance for less money from other brands. It’s also not ideal if you need massive ice output for large events. OK so it’ll work, but you’ll be babysitting it. ## Final verdict

The Frigidaire EFIC108 is a solid mid-range portable ice maker. Nothing about it blew me away, but nothing disappointed me either. It makes ice at a reasonable pace, it looks good, it’s easy to use, and it has a self-cleaning function that simplifies maintenance. OK so you’re paying about $110 for a machine that performs like an $80 machine but looks like a $150 one. Here’s the thing: the brand name and the stainless steel finish account for that gap. If those things matter to you, it’s a fair deal. If they don’t, there are cheaper options that’ll give you the same ice. Score: 7.5/10. Reliable, good-looking, slightly overpriced for what it does.
